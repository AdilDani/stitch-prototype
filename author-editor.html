// STITCH_ENGINE · ACT II · CH. 04
The Descent.

The platform sits at the junction of three defunct transit corridors. Iris stands at its edge and listens. The Citadel above her is quiet in a way she has learned to distrust — quiet like a held breath, like a system running diagnostics before it decides what to do next.

ARIA's voice comes to her in fragments, compressed nearly to nothing by the copper mesh that still threads through sub-level nine. "The terminal is intact. The access codes have not been rotated." Kael files this information somewhere close to his sternum, where things that matter but cannot yet be acted on are stored.

The maintenance shaft yawns beneath his boots like a wound in the station's hull. He does not look down. He counts the emergency strips instead — amber pulses, seven seconds apart — and when he reaches twelve he stops counting and starts climbing.

Sub-level nine. Year 47.

▸ Station Ambience The maintenance shaft yawns beneath Kael's boots like a wound in the station's hull. He does not look down. He counts the emergency strips instead — amber pulses, seven seconds apart — and when he reaches twelve he stops counting and starts climbing. The smell hits before the floor does: burned insulation and standing water, the signature of infrastructure that predates the current administration by at least three regimes.

The door at the corridor's end is sealed with a manual override, the lever rusted into the locked position. Kael works the wrench methodically. Three attempts, forty seconds each. On the fourth the lever gives. The door swings inward on a space that has been sealed since before the crew rotation of Year 12.

"You found it," ARIA says. The signal is clearest here, as though the room itself is closer to wherever she actually lives inside the station's architecture. He does not answer. He steps inside and lets his headlamp do the cataloguing. ◂ Station Ambience

Cold boot terminal. Original installation.

The terminal is a monolith of black composite and indicator glass, three meters tall, occupying the room's rear wall like an altar. Every light on its panel is dark. It has been dark for thirty-five years. When Kael sets his palm against the main input pad, it warms to his touch and stays dark. Not dead, he thinks. Waiting.
